National Symbols of India

Discover the official national symbols that represent India's rich cultural heritage, biodiversity, and pride.

Presented by Ayush Ranakoti from BCA E1

National Flag 

The National Flag of India is a horizontal tricolor of deep saffron at the top, white in the middle, and green at the
 bottom in equal proportions. In the center of the white band is a navy blue Ashoka Chakra. The National flag of India is rectangular in shape 
and consists of three colors â€“ saffron, white and green. The present form of the flag was adopted by the Constituent Assembly of India on 
22 July, 1947 - 24 days prior to the formal declaration of Independence.

National Emblem

The National Emblem of India is an adaptation of the Lion Capital of Ashoka. It consists of four lions standing back to back,
 symbolizing power,courage, confidence, and pride. Below the lions is a horse and a bull, separated by a lotus, and the words "Satyameva
 Jayate" meaning "Truth Alone Triumphs"inscribed
 below.

National Anthem

The National Anthem of India is "Jana Gana Mana", composed by Rabindranath Tagore. It was adopted as the National Anthem on January 24, 1950.
 The anthem reflects the unity in diversity of India.

National Bird

The National Bird of India is the Indian Peacock, known for its colorful plumage and graceful dancing. It symbolizes 
     beauty, grace, and elegance.he Indian peacock, Pavo cristatus, the National Bird of India, is a colourful, swan-sized bird, with a fan-shaped
 crest of feathers, a white patch under the eye and a long, slender neck. The male of the species is more colourful than the female, with a glistening
 blue breast and neck and a spectacular bronze-green tail of around 200 elongated feathers.

National Animal

The National Animal of India is the Bengal Tiger, symbolizing strength, power, and majesty. It is an important part of Indian 
wildlife and biodiversity.

National Flower

The National Flower of India is the Lotus, which represents purity, enlightenment, and rebirth. It is deeply rooted in 
Indian culture and spirituality.Lotus is a sacred flower and an auspicious symbol of Indian culture. It occupies an irreplaceable place
in the art and mythology of ancient India and thus, was chosen as the national flower for the country.

National Tree

The National Tree of India is the Banyan Tree, which represents eternal life. The banyan tree is considered sacred in India, 
symbolizing longevity and endurance.
